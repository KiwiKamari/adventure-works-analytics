## Revenue Concentration Among Consumers

<p align="center">
<img width="20%" height="20%" alt="imagen" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/64bd88e8-8daa-47fc-b1b3-f7adc23ae0f6" />

</p>

The Pareto analysis demonstrates that a relatively small percentage of B2C customers (27.88% - 5154 accounts) is responsible for the majority of total revenue generation.

This confirms a strong concentration of consumer spending within a limited customer group.

### Key Findings

- Revenue contribution is heavily concentrated among top customers.
- A minority of consumers generates most business revenue.
- Customer purchasing value distribution is highly uneven.

## Consumer Revenue Dependency

The combined RFM and Pareto analysis suggests that business performance depends disproportionately on a relatively limited number of high-value consumers.

This creates measurable concentration exposure despite the broader B2C customer base.

### Key Findings

- Revenue dependency on top consumers remains substantial.
- Customer diversification does not fully eliminate concentration risk.
- Retention of high-value consumers remains strategically important.
