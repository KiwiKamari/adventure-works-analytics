## Customer Distribution Across Segments

<p align="center">
<img width="60%" height="60%" alt="imagen" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/95bbc993-a20f-4db0-ba85-44befb7f686b" />
</p>

The segmentation analysis reveals that the B2B customer base is unevenly distributed across RFM groups, indicating substantial differences in purchasing activity, engagement, and customer value.

Certain segments contain a significantly larger share of customers, while high-value strategic groups remain comparatively concentrated.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> RFM models were computed exclusively using customers with transactional history available in `SalesOrderHeader`.
> Customers without purchases were intentionally excluded from Recency, Frequency, and Monetary calculations to preserve analytical validity.

### Key Findings

- Customer distribution is not balanced across segments.
- High-value customer groups represent a smaller portion of the customer base.
- Lower-engagement segments contain a considerable share of total customers.

## Revenue Concentration Among Strategic Accounts

<p align="center">
<img width="20%" height="20%" alt="imagen" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/7b9f3896-7cd1-460d-9511-f1fdaf454977" />

</p>

The Pareto analysis reveals that only 29.29% of B2B customers (186 accounts) generate 80% of total business revenue.

This indicates a highly concentrated revenue structure where a relatively small number of business clients drives the majority of commercial performance.

### Key Findings

- Revenue generation depends heavily on a limited number of strategic accounts.
- High-value B2B customers contribute disproportionately to total revenue.
- Customer concentration risk is significantly elevated.
- Retention of top-performing accounts is operationally critical.

---

## Revenue Dependency Risk

The combination of RFM segmentation and Pareto concentration reveals that business performance may depend heavily on a relatively small number of strategic accounts.

This introduces potential exposure to customer concentration risk.

### Key Findings

- Revenue dependency on top customers is substantial.
- Loss of key accounts could significantly affect business performance.
- Customer diversification opportunities may exist across lower-value segments.
