## Revenue Concentration Across Product Categories

The analysis reveals a strong revenue concentration within the Bikes category, which dominates overall sales volume compared to the remaining product groups.

This indicates that the business depends heavily on core bicycle products as its primary revenue driver.

At the same time, high revenue concentration also implies higher exposure to category-specific demand fluctuations.

## Cost Structure & Profitability Efficiency

<p align="center">
<img width="25%" height="25%" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/283c8ad1-9ca9-404f-bafc-ecab1b6eba72" />
</p>

Although Bikes generate the largest share of revenue, they also accumulate the highest operational costs.

The dashboard shows that revenue leadership does not always translate into superior profitability efficiency.

Some lower-volume categories demonstrate healthier margin behavior relative to their size.

### Key Findings

- High-revenue categories may operate with thinner margins.
- Smaller categories can contribute disproportionately strong profitability efficiency.
- Revenue alone is insufficient to evaluate business performance accurately.

## Product Pricing vs Margin Behavior

The relationship between product pricing and profitability is not strictly linear.

The scatter analysis suggests that premium-priced products do not necessarily achieve the highest margins, while several mid-priced products maintain stronger profitability ratios.

### Key Findings

- Higher prices do not automatically guarantee higher margin performance.
- Margin dispersion exists across multiple pricing segments.
- Certain mid-range products appear operationally more efficient than premium products.
